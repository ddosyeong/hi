Even though we don’t talk much in real life, I’ve come to like you more than I ever thought possible. I accept your imperfections, because you’ve accepted mine. Like you said, who are we to judge when we’re all flawed? I still remember November 29, 2024, when I confessed for the first time. I said it awkwardly, without preparation, but I meant every word.

You probably see your kindness as just a friendly gesture, and I understand that. But to me, it felt like something more—though I never let myself assume too much. You told me to try my best, no matter what the results. No one else believed in me like you did. I pressed my wrong buttons, made my mistakes, but you never gave up on me.

For a long time, I never thought I deserved your attention or your compliments, especially because I’m constantly compared to my sister at home. But for some reason, hearing it from you felt different—it stayed with me.

You always tried to be there for me in your own way, and I want you to know that it means the world. Even if I don’t say it often, I appreciate every little thing you do. It’s helped me grow into a better person, even when I didn’t realize it.

I’m grateful for the moments we’ve shared, even the quiet ones. Sometimes silence speaks louder than words, and in those moments, I felt a connection with you that I didn’t expect.

This letter might be long, but it’s only because there’s so much I want you to know. You’re special to me, and I hope this helps you understand how I feel.
